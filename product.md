# PRODUCT.md

## Product

Mu — The 10,000 Rep Journey

Mu is a personal mastery system built around the philosophy that greatness is the accumulation of thousands of intentional repetitions.

It is not a productivity app.
It is not a habit tracker.
It is a digital dojo.

## Primary User

A highly ambitious individual pursuing mastery in one or more difficult disciplines over years.

Typical users:

* Traders practicing execution.
* Athletes refining movement patterns.
* Artists and musicians accumulating deliberate practice.
* Programmers and builders developing technical expertise.

Most sessions happen alone, often late at night, in deep focus.

The product should feel like a quiet companion rather than a motivational coach.

## Core Purpose

Transform invisible effort into visible progress.

The app exists to answer one question:

"Did I show up and do the work today?"

## Emotional Outcome

After logging a rep, the user should feel:

* Quiet pride.
* Momentum.
* Calm satisfaction.
* A sense of ritual completed.

Never excitement.
Never dopamine farming.
Never social validation.

The reward is becoming the kind of person who returns tomorrow.

## Design Philosophy

The interface should feel like:

* A Japanese dojo.
* A luxury mechanical watch.
* A minimalist private library.
* A Bloomberg Terminal stripped of noise.

Keywords:

silence
precision
craftsmanship
discipline
patina
ritual
mastery
timelessness

## Anti-Goals

Do not make the product feel like:

* Duolingo
* Habitica
* Gamified productivity apps
* Startup SaaS dashboards
* Social media
* Crypto trading terminals

Avoid:

* Bright colors
* Excessive gradients
* Achievement explosions
* Confetti
* Large emojis
* Fake motivation

## Visual Language

Background:
Pure black and deep charcoal.

Accent:
Dark oxblood red.

Typography:
Elegant serif for identity.
Monospace for numbers and statistics.

Motion:
Slow.
Subtle.
Intentional.

Spacing:
Generous whitespace.
Every element should feel carefully placed.

## Success Metric

A user should be able to open the app after 1,000 consecutive days and feel:

"I've built something sacred."
